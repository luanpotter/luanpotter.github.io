## History

Recently I've been studying and understanding the new [Cricut Maker 4](https://cricut.com) that we got for the office. After a thorough intro by our resident expert, I started to wonder how well it would fare as a pipeline for printing prototype cards. It is an idea I had dabbled with before (some sort of automated cutting machine), but never really pursued it.

Previously, as I was experimenting with both my (still work-in-progress) TCG, [Dimensional Rift](https://luan.xyz/projects/dim-rift), and board game with cards, [Flaky Flasks](https://luan.xyz/projects/flaky-flasks), I'd either have the cards printed at the office, and cut them myself (through a myriad of manual tools) - which was an excruciating amount of work and resulted in terrible quality due to my thorough lack of any artistic ability; or I paid some pretty penny for the corner print shop - to mixed results in terms of final quality.

As I plan to iterate on those and other game development projects, and since also have other pursuits that require printing cards (trying out new cards and decks from established games, or first trials of online-only, print-and-play, or otherwise niche TCGs), I decided to give the Cricut a go. And to my surprise, it did not take too much finagling to get it working!
