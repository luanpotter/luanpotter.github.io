Summon

Summoning is one of the slow game actions players can perform during their Main Phase.

It means to "Invoke", "Cast" or "Activate" a card face-up onto the battlefield. Done either by:

(1) playing the card face-up from your hand or extra deck; or (2) flipping face-up an already played set card.

When a card is summoned, its Summoning Cost, if present, must be immediately paid. Failing to pay it will fizzle the summon and cause the card to be sent to the graveyard (without triggering "When destroyed" effects).

This is the order of operations to follow:

  • reveal the card to both players, and select which zone it will be summoned to
  • chose the desired orientation
  • physically position the card on the selected zone in the chosen orientation
  • collect Mana Counters on the card if it has any Knowledge
  • activated any "When summoned" effects

Note that the order of operations has a few extra steps in case of a Flip summon.

Summoning is the "opposite" of Set, which means playing a card from your hand face-down.
